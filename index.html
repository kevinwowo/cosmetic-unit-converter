Cosmetic Unit Converter

Convert ml, cc, US fl oz, tsp, tbsp, grams and ounces — with a density field for accurate volume-to-weight.

Density matters when converting between volume and weight. Water & toner ≈ 1.0, lotions & creams ≈ 0.9–1.05, oils ≈ 0.91–0.93.

How to use this converter

Enter an amount and pick the unit you are starting from. To convert between a volume (ml, cc, fl oz) and a weight (grams, oz), set the product density — otherwise the tool assumes water-like 1.0 g/ml. For a cream filled by weight but specified in ml, the density is what makes the two line up.

FAQ

Is ml the same as cc?
Yes. 1 ml = 1 cc = 1 cm³. They are identical for liquids.
How many ml in a US fluid ounce?
1 US fl oz = 29.5735 ml. So a 1 fl oz cosmetic bottle holds about 29.6 ml.
How do I convert ml to grams for a cosmetic?
Multiply the volume in ml by the density in g/ml. 50 ml of a cream at 0.95 g/ml weighs about 47.5 g.
Why do my fill weight and fill volume not match?
Most cosmetics are not water. If the density is below 1.0 the product weighs less than its volume in ml; above 1.0 it weighs more. Always use the actual density.
